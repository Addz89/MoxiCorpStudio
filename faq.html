Project basics

A smaller Starter Pack can often move faster because there are fewer pages and fewer decisions. Larger Growth and Elite builds take more time because they include more content, stronger page structure, deeper polish, animations, SEO foundations, testing, and revisions.

You should provide your business name, logo if you have one, services, preferred colours, photos, contact details, social links, examples of websites you like, and any existing website or domain information. If you do not have all of that, the site can still be planned properly.

No. One-page websites can work for simple offers, but most real businesses need multiple pages: home, about, services, pricing, FAQ, contact, portfolio, and sometimes landing pages. A proper structure gives visitors more trust and gives search engines more content to understand.

02

Payments and Stripe

Yes. Stripe checkout buttons can be connected to your Starter, Growth, and Elite packages so clients can pay securely online. This makes your business look more professional and removes messy back-and-forth payment handling.

Payment terms can depend on the project size. For smaller fixed-price packs, upfront payment can keep the build simple. For larger projects, a deposit and final payment structure may make more sense. Clear payment terms should always be confirmed before work starts.

The pack price should cover the agreed website build. Extra pages, extra design work, logo creation, advanced copywriting, maintenance, paid plugins, domain registration, hosting, and third-party platform costs should be quoted separately so there are no surprises.

03

Website features

Yes. Mobile responsiveness is not optional. Most people will judge a business from their phone first, so the layout, spacing, buttons, text, sections, and forms must work cleanly across phone, tablet, laptop, and desktop screens.

Basic SEO foundations are included in the structure: page titles, headings, content layout, metadata guidance, image alt text, speed-focused design, and clean navigation. Bigger packs can include stronger page depth and better content sections for search visibility.

Yes — but animations need discipline. Flashy movement that slows the site down is trash. The goal is professional motion: reveal effects, hover states, device mockups, animated cards, smooth transitions, and micro-interactions that make the website feel premium without hurting performance.

04

After launch

Yes. The site can be prepared for launch and connected to the chosen domain and hosting setup. Domain and hosting accounts usually remain under the business owner's control, which is the correct setup because the business should own its own digital assets.

Yes. New pages, text changes, image swaps, extra sections, landing pages, SEO improvements, speed checks, and ongoing maintenance can be quoted as add-ons or handled through a support arrangement.
